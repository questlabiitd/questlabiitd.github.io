Performance Assessment of Low-Cost PM Sensors under Diverse Exposure and Meteorological Conditions

Gulshan Kumar

Supervisors: Jay Dhariwal & Seshan Srirangarajan

This study investigates spatial and temporal variations of PM2.5 levels in Kareri Lake region. Using portable sensing systems, we evaluate environmental influences and assess implications for air quality monitoring in remote high-altitude regions.

Research and Developmentof a novel video laryngoscope for endotracheal intubation in Indian population

Harshit Mourya

Supervisors: Jay Dhariwal, Kaushik Mukherjee & Nishkarsh Gupta

This research focuses on understanding upper airway anatomy to inform the design of novel laryngoscope blade shapes derived from airway profile analysis. It includes the development of an integrated pressure sensing mechanism to provide real-time feedback during intubation. The work also emphasizes ergonomic design and biomechanical evaluation to ensure usability and safety. The overall aim is to create a clinically relevant, anatomically adaptive video laryngoscope system.

Designing a climate-responsive framework for constructing a Heat Vulnerability Index (HVI)

Sonal Gangrade

Supervisors: Jay Dhariwal

This study investigates the impact of indoor environmental quality (IEQ) on occupant health and wellness. We evaluate factors such as air quality, lighting, and thermal comfort to understand their influence on well-being in built environments.

Indoor airborne transmission under outdoor PM2.5 infiltration

Vamsi Krishna Bankapalli

Supervisors: Jay Dhariwal & Narsing Kumar Jha

Ventilation is a potential strategy to reduce the probability of indoor airborne infections. However, in polluted cities, this strategy is contradicted by the infiltration of outdoor PM2.5. This work aims to map different strategies to optimize both these through large-scale field experiments, lab-scale experiments, and reduced-order modelling.

Estimation of Indoor Airborne Pathogen Concentration

Animesh Kumar Vyas

Supervisors: Jay Dhariwal & Vikram Singh

Our research investigates airborne pathogen transmission dynamics in high-risk settings, specifically AIIMS healthcare facilities and IIT Delhi classrooms. By combining real-world air sampling with mathematical infection risk models, we estimate exposure risks to better transmission physics and epidemiology. Ultimately, this work aims to inform more effective environmental controls to protect individuals in vulnerable indoor spaces.

Design and Development of Low-Cost Devices for Carbonaceous Aerosol Monitoring

Prasannaa Kumar D

Supervisors: Jay Dhariwal & Seshan Srirangarajan

This research focuses on the development of low-cost, high-resolution instruments for measuring black carbon (BC) in diverse environmental conditions. The work integrates optical sensing based on the aethalometer principle with adaptive sampling strategies to improve measurement accuracy and extend filter life under high particulate loads.

Wireless-Enabled Surgical Workflows in Minimally Invasive Surgery

D Bavath

Supervisors: Jay Dhariwal & Dinesh Kalyanasundaram

Minimally invasive surgery (MIS) relies on wired endoscopic systems where rigid cables restrict instrument maneuverability and limit the surgeon’s freedom of movement. These constraints often require two-handed adjustments for simple tasks, leading to inefficient handling, increased physical strain, and interruptions in visual feedback. Additionally, cables contribute to recurring costs due to sterilization, maintenance, and frequent replacements. They also occupy significant space in the operating environment, creating clutter and complicating workflow. This study investigates these limitations and proposes a wireless endoscopic solution to improve mobility, efficiency, and overall surgical performance.
